H711, Hip Hop Computer Game/VJ Software . English, German, Spanish, French, Song Collaborators: MC Bala aka Carolina Caycedo, Rummenigger Puttabong, Elke Marhoefer and Parangari Cutiri. Performed in Wooshi/Unsonar, Barcelona 2003, Schloss Solitude, Stuttgart 2003, Centro de la Imagen, Mexico City 2003, Killer Instinct, New Museum, NY 2003, 1K Limited Edition published by Merz and Solitude 2004 (ISBN 3-929085-98-4) with support from f30km/s Foundation.

A two player music game where players must remix songs and rhyme well in order to score more points. Algorithimic visuals programmed by Parangari Cutiri coincide with beats created by four all female guest hip hop musicians from various countries. CD includes booklet with the essay: "Moving to Other Beats: Politics of Rhythm, Worldwide Hip Hop and the Computer Game, HeavenSeven11" translated into German, French and Spanish from English. CD also includes quicktime footage of Carolina Caycedo and MCBala and Anne-Marie Schleiner aka Parangari Cutiri performing live at the New Museum, NY. Full length unaltered songs also included on CD. Game plays on Mac9.0, MacOSX and Windows. CD jacket design by Marco Noris.
