Arts 5117 Grad Seminar: Special Focus: Collaboration vs. Competition: an Art Reality Challenge
Instructor: Anne-Marie Schleiner, email: opensorcery@opensorcery.net, Arts Rm 271, Class Times Weds: Office Hours by appt.

This seminar will challenge graduate students of fine art with pressing questions about the nature of art practice, the role of the artist and the art professional. We will look at various traditional and more recent models for art making currently under revision in the art world and in M.F.A. programs such the studio, the research lab, music-making, interdisciplinary practice and collaboration. We will pose the question of who is the art audience for artwork? Should art address the art world and/or non artists or specific targeted communities? We will consider the advantages and disadvantages of working within the gallery system of museums, galleries and collectors vs. other options such as public artmaking and collaborative online art. Students will develop critical and professional skills in presenting, critiquing, and contextualizing their own artwork. They will also experiment beyond the limitations of themselves as individual artists while developing a collaborative class project.

Projects:

#1 Art Presentation: Who you and your art are and what it is related to:
Option a: Contextualize your work to date within the movements and artists of the 20th + century and present one hour to class.
Option b: Impersonate another artist in the class and present and contextualize their work
Option c: Write an art manifesto for yourself and your contemporaries and present it to the class
#2 Artist Promotion: Target a local Denver gallery after attending First Friday that may be interested in your work and submit work to them with one page artist statement and (slides, video, etc as appropriate) or apply to an art residency anywhere in the world
#3 Collaborative Class Project: Reality Art Game with Two Challenges
One online challenge and one in person challenge with guest artists, students design challenges for art reality challenge, document with video and post on the web
